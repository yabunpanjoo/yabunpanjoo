Common questions

Do participants have to bring anything?
  • Clothing for camp duration inclusive of hiking footwear.
  • Sleeping Bag and Pillow.
  • Toiletries, towel, and personal products.
  • Dishes/Mess Kit, Cup and Water Bottle.
  • Hat, Sunscreen, and Insect Repellent.
  • Headlamp and/or Flashlight plus spare batteries.

¬†

All other items are supplied.

Will Food Be Provided On The Camps?
  • Food will be provided for you on The Short and Long Camps.
  • Fresh food supplies will be purchased from local towns daily.
  • We cater for vegetarians, vegans, and those affected by food allergies, please let us know
  • If attending a short camp, fruit and water will be supplied, however we advise you bring your own lunch.

¬†

Are the camps physically demanding?
  • There is no demanding physical activity on the camps.
Are any emergency procedures in place for the camps?
  • A safety vehicle will always remain onsite and be fully fuelled for emergencies.
  • The Short and Long Camps have a First Aider.

¬†

Are there toilet and shower facilities on the camps?
  • There is onsite toilets and shower facilities.

¬†

Anything else to know?
  • Medications will be the responsibility of individuals or chaperones of children.
  • Elders and others all hold current Working with Children Blue Cards.
  • Within the Short Camp there will be some opportunities for participants to go exploring or swimming etc, alternatively, the Long Camp has many opportunities for these.
