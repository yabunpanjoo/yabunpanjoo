We Are Yabun Panjoo

Embark on an enriching voyage with Yabun Panjoo, where we extend an invitation to all to uncover and truly embrace the intricate tapestry of Indigenous culture that has intricately woven itself through our nation’s heart for countless millennia. Anchored in ancient traditions yet enlivened by the contemporary spirit, Yabun Panjoo stands as an entrance to the captivating realm of Australia’s First Nations culture.

Through a harmonious fusion of storytelling, art, music, and knowledge, our aim is to cultivate a more profound comprehension of the world’s oldest continuous culture, all while paying tribute to the resilience of Indigenous communities across the Australian landscape. Yabun Panjoo serves as a collaborative haven, rejoicing in the rich cultural heritage of this land with utmost pride, and gifting that knowledge to all.

Join us on this inspiring odyssey, a shared celebration and exposition of the ingenuity, and elegance of Indigenous Australia. United, we have the potential to erect bridges of connection, forging a brighter future that is firmly rooted in an unshakable reverence for the past.

Our mission

Yabun Panjoo stands as a radiant beacon of unity and harmony, fuelled by a core purpose to unite individuals from diverse backgrounds. Firmly rooted in the conviction that diversity is a wellspring of strength and should be celebrated, Yabun Panjoo is dedicated to cultivating mutual comprehension and reverence among people from all walks of life. The organization’s initiatives and campaigns orbit around constructing connections between communities, nurturing authentic friendships, and dismantling obstacles that impede social cohesion.

At the very core of Yabun Panjoo’s philosophy lies the acknowledgment that authentic progress materializes when individuals of varied cultures, ethnicities, and life experiences converge with open minds and hearts imbued with empathy. Through dynamic workshops, enlightening educational programs, and invigorating dialogue sessions, Yabun Panjoo facilitates candid conversations and profound exchanges. Participants are encouraged to share their narratives, perspectives, and cultural traditions, thus deepening their recognition of the tapestry of human experiences, and expand their horizons.

In the heart of Yabun Panjoo’s endeavours lies the aspiration to engender a sense of belonging and camaraderie within the community. By orchestrating all-encompassing events and activities, the organization ensures that each person feels esteemed and enveloped in acceptance for their distinct identity. In such a nurturing environment, individuals are emboldened to present their genuine selves, fostering a robust communal spirit where all individual’s contributions are acknowledged and commemorated.

Our Approach

Yabun Panjoo’s approach extends beyond mere tolerance; it actively encourages the formation of lasting friendships that span cultural and social boundaries. By bringing individuals together for shared experiences, whether through art, music, dance, or communal projects, Yabun Panjoo nurtures genuine connections. These friendships go beyond surface-level interactions, breaking down stereotypes and promoting authentic appreciation for one another’s humanity.

In its unwavering commitment to promoting social cohesion, Yabun Panjoo identifies and addresses barriers that can hinder the path to unity. Through targeted initiatives and awareness campaigns, the organization tackles issues such as language barriers, economic disparities, cultural misunderstandings, and prejudice. By acknowledging and challenging these obstacles, Yabun Panjoo seeks to create a more inclusive and equitable society where everyone has equal opportunities to thrive and contribute to the collective progress.

Yabun Panjoo stands as a testament to the transformative power of embracing diversity. It showcases the beauty of unity in diversity, highlighting how coming together with openness and compassion can lead to positive change and a more harmonious community. As Yabun Panjoo continues to lead with passion, dedication, and inclusivity, it inspires us all to recognize the inherent value of every individual and work together for a future where respect, understanding, and friendship form the bedrock of a vibrant and united society.

Our Dedicated Elders and Volunteers

Uncle Russell “Tassie” Butler

Uncle Russ is a Senior Traditional Elder of the Bandjin and Warragamay people of the Hinchinbrook Island region between Cairns and Townsville. As a boy, Russell learned many traditional skills and stories from his grandmother. “I went to two schools — mainstream school and my grandmother’s school,” he says. “Now it’s my job to pass on this knowledge to keep those skills and traditions active.” He specialises in teaching the cultural and material aspects of the traditional Aboriginal lifestyle in North Queensland from the coast to the rainforest. Uncle Russ uses stories, discussions, and bush walks to teach ethnobotany, tool making, and Aboriginal environmental philosophies.

Uncle Wayne Parker

An Aboriginal man from Kalkadoon Country, Mount Isa, Uncle Wayne creates a difference for young people in community through sporting programs such as boxing. With Indigenous-designed and led community programs, kids can grow up in an environment that nurtures their potential rather than being trapped in the justice system. Uncle Wayne has been a cohort of Uncle Russ for many years and possesses a strong spirit that offers his knowledge of the past from a more contemporary and deeper community-embedded perspective that resonates with campers from all levels of life.

Uncle Dave “Gugila”

A proud Dunghutti and adopted Bandjin man, Uncle Dave has spent over 35 years mentoring, engaging, and coaching our next generations. He was a Rugby League Junior and Senior Coach for 34 years finishing this aspect in Townsville in 2019. He holds the ambassadorial role of 3rd Brigade Uncle at Lavarack Barracks as a Warrant Officer and enjoys conducting Aboriginal ceremonies to excite and inculcate this with all peoples. As a Trainer & Assessor by trade, he has proven himself to be a wonderful portrayer of information in a style that is engaging and entertaining. He quotes Uncle Russ and Uncle Bim as two of the most important people he has ever known.
